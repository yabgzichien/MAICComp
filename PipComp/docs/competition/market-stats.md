# Pip Credit — Market Statistics & Sources

Verified data for the pitch deck, project summary, and Q&A. **Last verified: 2026-06-04.**
Each figure lists its primary attribution and any caveat — use the caveats so a judge can't
puncture a number. Prefer the **bold** headline figures on slides.

## 1. The problem / market size

| Figure | Stat | Source (primary) | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RM90 billion** | Estimated financing gap for **micro SMEs** in Malaysia | Securities Commission Malaysia, **Capital Market Masterplan 3 (CMP3), 2021** | SC estimate, ~2021 vintage; still widely cited in 2024–25. Attribute to "SC / CMP3," not to BNM. |
| ~97% | MSMEs as share of all business establishments | **DOSM, MSME Performance 2024** | Sometimes cited as 96.1% (older/definition-dependent). |
| **39.5%** | MSME contribution to GDP (RM652.4bn value added, 2024) | **DOSM, MSME Performance 2024** | **NOT 60%** — a secondary source overstated this; use 39.5%. |
| 48.7% | MSME share of national employment (8.10m persons, 2024) | **DOSM, MSME Performance 2024** | — |
| ~88–90% | Adult bank-account ownership in Malaysia | World Bank Global Findex (2021) | Why "unbanked" is the wrong frame; the gap is *credit invisibility*, not access to an account. |

## 2. The customers already exist (B2B demand)

| Figure | Stat | Source | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **>RM5 billion** | Govt micro-financing allocated to **400,000+** small/micro businesses, via AIM, BSN, TEKUN, MARA, Agrobank, Bank Rakyat | The Edge Malaysia | Recurring annual budget allocation; confirm latest year before quoting. |
| Group lending | AIM & TEKUN use 5-person **joint-liability** lending — i.e. they cannot individually credit-assess borrowers | SmallBusinessLoanMY (AIM/TEKUN scheme guides) | Secondary; the group-lending model itself is well established (AIM = Grameen-style since 1987). |
| Dec 2024 | **MDEC + CGC Digital + PayNet** collaboration to close the RM90bn gap **"through alternative data for credit scoring"** | The Edge / press coverage | Directly validates Pip Credit's method; MDEC also endorses MAIC Nexus — potential partner/customer. |

## 3. Approach validation

| Item | Why it matters | Source |

## 4. Regulatory tailwind

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumer Credit Act 2025** | Gazetted Dec 2025; in force 1 Mar 2026. Creates the **Consumer Credit Commission (CCC)**. Licensing for credit providers (incl. BNPL) live **1 Jun 2026**, with 6 months to comply | The Edge / Fintech News Malaysia | 
| Implication | Newly-regulated lenders now legally need **defensible, auditable affordability assessment** — i.e. explainable scoring like Pip Credit's | — |

## 5. Competitive signal (comparable hackathon)

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **TNG FINHACK 2026** (Apr 2026) | Financial inclusion = **35.5%** of submissions (most crowded theme); security/fraud 33.9%. The **grand-prize winner was a fraud solution**, not a credit score | Digital News Asia / Fintech News Malaysia |
